CELL LIFE CYCLE CROWD-BASED ASSESSMENT

My name is Nathaniel Oteng. As part of my MS thesis advised by Professor Bart Geerts at the University of Wyoming, I am seeking expert volunteers to participate in a meteorological pattern recognition survey. The objective of this survey is crowd-based assessment of convection lifecycle based on first principles. The convection studied here is maritime convection of mediocre depth (4-5 km) that forms when cold air moves over relatively warm water. The key data source is the profiling Ka-band ARM Zenith Radar (KAZR) deployed on the northern Norwegian coast as part of the Cold-air Outbreak in the Marine Boundary Layer Experiment (COMBLE) (Geerts et al. 2022).

This lifecycle classification has two parts: one is observational analysis (Part 1), the other use the LES model output (Part 2). For Part 1, we have objectively identified 57 cells from KAZR profiles during an intense Cold-Air Outbreak on 13 March 2020. An additional dataset is from a scanning C-band radar network, which depicts horizontal cloud structure and evolution. For Part 2, we use a large-domain large-eddy simulation (LES) with a resolution of 30 m and 150 m over the entire Norwegian Sea from the Arctic Sea down to northern Scandinavia (Juliano et al. 2024). 52 cells were identified in the LES output. More details will be provided in the survey.
